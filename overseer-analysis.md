# HORIZON EUROPE - Work Programme 2025-2027 HORIZON-CL4-2025-DIGITAL-01-03 Analysis

## 1. Call Requirements Summary
- Programme: Horizon Europe
- Reference Number: HORIZON-CL4-2025-DIGITAL-01-03
- Topic: Trustworthy Artificial Intelligence for Resilient Digital Infrastructure and Public Services
- Type of Action: Research and Innovation Action (RIA)
- Funding Rate: 100% of eligible costs
- Budget Envelope: EUR 8,000,000 (indicative: 2 projects)
- Max EU contribution per project: EUR 4,000,000
- Project Duration: 36 months
- Opening Date: 15 January 2025
- Deadline: 23 September 2025, 17:00 Brussels time
- Submission Procedure: Electronic submission via the EU Funding & Tenders Portal

## 2. Strategic Opportunities
- Top priority 1: Development of explainable AI (XAI) methods for public services
- Top priority 2: Creation of privacy-preserving AI techniques that comply with GDPR
- Top priority 3: Building trustworthy, explainable, and resilient AI systems across various sectors
- Innovation angle: Advancements in XAI, federated learning, differential privacy, and real-time threat detection
- Gaps in the current landscape: Limited trustworthiness and interpretability of AI systems; insufficient focus on GDPR compliance in AI research
- Preferred consortium type: Mixed (academic, SMEs, NGOs, government)
- Budget sweet spot: EUR 4,000,000 per project

## 3. Proposed Project Concept
### Title: TRUSTEDAI - Enhancing Trustworthy Artificial Intelligence for Europe's Digital Infrastructure and Public Services
### Acronym: TRUSTEDAI
### Core Problem: Lack of trustworthiness, explainability, and resilience in AI systems deployed across critical digital infrastructure and public services
### Innovative Solution: Development of cutting-edge XAI methods, privacy-preserving techniques, and real-time threat detection frameworks
### Main Objective: Develop novel explainable AI methods for high-stakes decision support in public services, ensuring outputs are interpretable by non-technical stakeholders
#### Specific Objectives:
1. Develop explainable AI (XAI) methods suitable for high-stakes decision support in public services
2. Design and validate AI-driven cybersecurity monitoring frameworks that detect and respond to threats in real time across distributed digital infrastructure
3. Create privacy-preserving AI techniques (e.g., federated learning, differential privacy) that comply with GDPR while maintaining model performance
4. Build open-source AI toolkits and benchmarks for trustworthiness assessment
5. Demonstrate the developed solutions through at least two real-world pilot deployments in different EU Member States
6. Contribute to European standardisation bodies (CEN/CENELEC, ETSI) with at least one standards-track document submitted during the project lifetime
7. Provide policy recommendations for AI governance in public services, validated with at least 2 national or regional public authorities
#### Key Outputs:
1. XAI methodologies and frameworks
2. Real-time cybersecurity monitoring tools
3. Privacy-preserving AI techniques (e.g., federated learning, differential privacy)
4. Open-source AI toolkits and benchmarks for trustworthiness assessment
5. Policy recommendations for AI governance in public services
6. Standards-track documents submitted to CEN/CENELEC, ETSI
7. Two real-world pilot deployments in EU Member States
#### Expected Impacts:
1. Increased trust in AI-based public services
2. Improved cyber-resilience of critical digital infrastructure
3. At least 3 open-source tools or libraries published under permissive licenses (Apache 2.0 or equivalent) and registered in the EU AI-on-Demand platform
4. Policy recommendations for AI governance in public services, validated with at least 2 national or regional public authorities
5. Contribution to European standardisation bodies (CEN/CENELEC, ETSI)
6. Strengthened Europe's position as a global leader in trustworthy AI
7. Reduced dependency on non-European AI technology
8. Support for the uptake of AI in public administration while safeguarding fundamental rights

## 4. Consortium Design
- Number of partners: 5
- Required expertise areas: AI, cybersecurity, privacy, policy, standardization
- Suggested partner types: Universities (research), SMEs (AI technology development), NGOs/Government bodies (policy, standardization)
- Country distribution: At least three EU Member States or Horizon Europe Associated Countries
- Coordinator profile: University with expertise in AI and cybersecurity research

## 5. Work Package Architecture
### WP1: Project Management and Coordination
### WP2: Develop Explainable AI Methods for High-Stakes Decision Support
#### Tasks:
1. Literature review on XAI methods
2. Propose novel methodologies for high-stakes decision support in public services
3. Validate proposed XAI methods using pilot deployments
### WP3: Real-time Threat Detection and Response Frameworks
#### Tasks:
1. Review existing AI-driven cybersecurity monitoring frameworks
2. Design and develop a real-time threat detection and response system for distributed digital infrastructure
3. Validate the proposed framework using simulations and pilot deployments
### WP4: Privacy-Preserving Techniques Compliant with GDPR
#### Tasks:
1. Review existing privacy-preserving AI techniques
2. Develop novel privacy-preserving methods compliant with GDPR
3. Integrate these methods into the frameworks developed in WP2 and WP3
### WP5: Open-Source Toolkits and Benchmarks for Trustworthiness Assessment
#### Tasks:
1. Create open-source toolkits for trustworthiness assessment (fairness, robustness, transparency, accountability)
2. Develop benchmarks to evaluate the trustworthiness of AI systems
3. Validate these tools and benchmarks through pilot deployments and comparisons with existing solutions
### WP6: Real-World Pilot Deployments in EU Member States
#### Tasks:
1. Identify and engage end-user organisations from different EU Member States
2. Deploy the developed frameworks in real-world settings
3. Evaluate the performance of these systems in the pilot deployments
### WP7: Exploitation, Dissemination, and Communication
#### Tasks:
1. Develop a comprehensive exploitation plan for commercialization opportunities
2. Create a public project website operational within 2 months of project start
3. Engage with the European AI community through workshops, webinars, and the AI-on-Demand platform
4. Publish at least 6 peer-reviewed publications in high-impact journals or conferences
5. Develop at least 2 policy briefs targeting EU and national policy-makers
6. Regularly update the public on project progress through social media
### WP8: Ethics, Legal, and Social Implications (ELSI)
#### Tasks:
1. Conduct ethics assessments for the entire project, including GDPR compliance and data protection impact assessment
2. Develop an algorithmic bias detection and mitigation strategy
3. Implement human oversight mechanisms in AI-assisted decision-making
4. Align with the EU Ethics Guidelines for Trustworthy AI
5. Monitor the project's progress against EU AI Act risk categories
### WP9: Project Management, Quality Assurance, and Risk Management
#### Tasks:
1. Coordinate the activities of all partners
2. Ensure timely delivery of milestones and deliverables
3. Conduct regular quality assurance reviews of project outputs
4. Identify and mitigate critical risks to project success
### WP10: Ethics Advisory Board
#### Tasks:
1. Establish an ethics advisory board with at least one external member
2. Regularly consult the advisory board on ethical considerations throughout the project
3. Incorporate their feedback into the project's decision-making processes
### WP11: Financial and Administrative Management
#### Tasks:
1. Prepare budget proposals, reports, and invoices as required by the funding agency
2. Manage subcontracting agreements with third parties
3. Ensure proper financial reporting and compliance with EU regulations
### WP12: Project Closure and Evaluation
#### Tasks:
1. Conduct a final project evaluation to assess the achievements, challenges, and lessons learned
2. Prepare a final report summarizing the project's results, impact, and recommendations
3. Organize a final conference or workshop to present the project's outcomes

## 6. Risk and Compliance Flags
- Eligibility risks: Limited experience in AI, cybersecurity, privacy, policy, standardization; insufficient consortium diversity (e.g., geography, expertise)
- Ethical considerations: GDPR compliance, algorithmic bias detection and mitigation, human oversight mechanisms, alignment with the EU Ethics Guidelines for Trustworthy AI
- Technical risks: Developing novel explainable AI methods, real-time threat detection frameworks, privacy-preserving techniques compliant with GDPR
- Critical success factors: Collaboration between partners, integration of developed tools and methodologies into pilot deployments, effective exploitation and dissemination plan
